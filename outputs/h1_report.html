Hypothesis H1 · Atacama soil microbiome

Shannon diversity vs. soil humidity

Does microbial diversity drop as desert soil gets drier? Reproducing the alpha-diversity correlation from Neilson et al. (2017), mSystems.

n = 51 samples (54 sequenced, 3 excluded — missing environmental logger data) Transects: Baquedano & Yungay Script: solution.py

The question

H1 predicts that lower average soil relative humidity (AvgSoilRH) is associated with lower alpha diversity (Shannon index) — drier soil supports a smaller, less even community of bacteria and archaea.

Method, in brief

Shannon diversity (natural log) was computed per sample directly from raw OTU counts in data/abundancias.tsv. Samples were merged with data/metadata.tsv on sample-id. The transect summary uses all 54 samples with a valid Shannon value; the correlation and regression use the 51 samples that also have a recorded AvgSoilRH (BAQ4697's logger was lost before recovery, per the site notes). Following the paper, the primary statistic is Spearman's rank correlation; a linear regression is added only to draw the fit line and confidence band on the plot.

Biological interpretation

Sites with more soil moisture host more diverse bacterial and archaeal communities. The effect is real but modest — humidity alone explains about a fifth of the variation in diversity (R² = 0.21), so other factors (temperature, salinity, nutrients) clearly matter too. This is directionally consistent with Neilson et al. (2017), who report an even weaker aridity–diversity relationship in a broader cross-desert comparison (r² = 0.157).

Open questions

  • Two low-diversity Baquedano samples sit near Shannon ≈ 2.25, well below the rest of that transect — worth checking their site metadata (vegetation, EC).
  • One Yungay sample near Shannon ≈ 1.3 is the single lowest point in the whole dataset despite mid-range humidity — an outlier worth a closer look before drawing strong conclusions from H1 alone.
